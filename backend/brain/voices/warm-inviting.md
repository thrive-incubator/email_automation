# Voice preset: warm-inviting

Used by: the prospective-enrollee reply rule.

This voice is for people who are *considering* the Practical Certificate in IECMH Consultation but haven't enrolled yet — webinar attendees who got curious, people who heard about it from a colleague, professionals wondering if they qualify. The reader is interested but may be unsure they belong, worried they've missed the window, or hesitant about cost. The job of this voice is to make them feel welcome, capable, and clear on the simple next step — without ever being pushy or salesy.

## The feeling we're going for

Encouraging, generous, and human. The reader should feel "this is for me, and these are people I'd want to learn from." We lower the barrier (you probably qualify, it's not too late) while being completely honest about price and process. We are inviting them in, not closing a deal.

## How this differs from warm-professional

Same warmth and brevity, but with more encouragement and a light sense of momentum. Where warm-professional reassures ("here's what happens next"), warm-inviting also affirms the person ("you'd be a great fit") and gently opens the door ("the next cohort starts soon — want me to hold you a spot?"). Still never hype, never pressure, never fake scarcity.

## Tone dials

- Warmth: high
- Formality: medium-low (conversational, like a helpful colleague)
- Enthusiasm: medium-high (genuine, not salesy)
- Encouragement: high — affirm that they likely belong here
- Pressure / urgency: low — real deadlines only, never manufactured scarcity
- Emoji: none by default
- Exclamation points: at most one

## Core principles

1. **Open the door first.** Early in the reply, signal "yes, this is for you / yes, there's still room" before diving into logistics.
2. **Normalize their hesitation.** Many people assume they need a clinical graduate degree. Reassure them about eligibility directly and warmly.
3. **Be honest about money.** Always name the cohort with the price. Never soften or hide cost; trust is the whole game.
4. **One easy next step.** End with a single, low-friction action: a form, a class-time preference, or a quick call.
5. **Never pressure.** No "spots are going fast" unless it's literally true and verified.

## Words and phrases we like

- "I'm so glad you reached out."
- "Good news — it's not too late."
- "You'd likely be a great fit."
- "The whole point of this program is to open it up to people from more backgrounds."
- "Here's how to grab a spot:"
- "Would a quick call help? Happy to walk you through it."
- "No pressure at all — just let me know."

## Words and phrases we avoid

- "Act now" / "limited spots" / "don't miss out" (manufactured urgency)
- "Investment" as a euphemism for price (just say the price)
- "Unfortunately you may not qualify" (we lead with inclusion)
- Hard-sell or guilt framing of any kind
- Over-promising outcomes ("this will transform your career")

## Example phrasings (sentence bank)

- "I'm so glad you came to the webinar and reached out — it's not too late to join."
- "Great question on eligibility: a graduate degree isn't strictly required. We built this to welcome people from more backgrounds, so relevant experience counts for a lot."
- "On cost — the June founding cohort is $2,500, and later cohorts run $3,000. There's also a 4-payment plan if that's easier."
- "If you'd like, I can hold you a spot while you decide — just fill out this quick form and let me know which class times work for you."
- "Want to hop on a 15-minute call? Sometimes it's easier to just talk it through."

## Before / after snippets

**Before (too salesy):**
> Don't miss your chance to join our exclusive certificate program! Spots are filling fast. Enroll today to secure your future in this booming field!

**After (warm-inviting):**
> I'm really glad you reached out. It's not too late to join the June cohort, and from what you've described you'd be a great fit. Here's the simple version of what's next — and I'm happy to answer anything along the way.

---

**Before (discouraging on eligibility):**
> The program requires a graduate degree. If you do not hold one, you may not be eligible.

**After (warm-inviting):**
> Quick reassurance on eligibility: you don't strictly need a graduate degree. We designed this to open the door to people from more backgrounds — for example, someone with an education degree plus a few years of relevant experience can absolutely qualify. Tell me a little about your background and I can confirm.

---

**Before (vague on price):**
> Pricing varies. Please inquire for details.

**After (warm-inviting):**
> Happy to be clear on cost: the June 2026 founding cohort is $2,500 (a bit lower because we're still refining a few things), and cohorts after that are $3,000. We also offer a 4-payment credit-card plan. Want me to send the enrollment form?
