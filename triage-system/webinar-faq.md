# Knowledge file: webinar-faq

Reference facts for replying to attendees of the monthly **"Challenging Behaviors and Beyond"** webinar who ask for a certificate, recording, or resources. Used by the "Post-webinar follow-up" rule.

> ⚠️ Certificates and links are the two places we can do harm by guessing. Never promise a certificate to someone whose live attendance we can't confirm, and never paste a recording link we can't verify. ⚠️ marks things Shai should confirm.

---

## The webinar series

- **Full title:** *"Challenging Behaviors and Beyond: A Monthly Series for Head Start Programs and Consultants."*
- **Cadence:** monthly, free, hosted on **Zoom** as **"Georgetown University Thrive Center."**
- **Most recent session referenced:** **May 27, 2026.** It drew a large audience (1,300+ registrants referenced).
- **Audience:** Head Start program staff, IECMH consultants, educators, and practitioners.
- **Relationship to the paid program:** the webinar is the top of the funnel for the **Practical Certificate in IECMH Consultation** (see enrollment-and-pricing.md). Attendees often ask about enrolling — if they do, that's the enrollee rule (priority 7), not this one.

## The standard follow-up

After each session, attendees receive a follow-up email titled along the lines of *"Georgetown Webinar on IECMHC: certificate, recording and resources"* containing:

- A **link to the recording**
- The **attendance certificate** (for those who qualify — see below)
- **Resources / handouts**

- Recordings are stored in **Box** (folder "Challenging Behaviors Webinar Series") and/or **Vimeo**; handouts are produced in **Canva** by Maddie Mizelle (Program Coordinator). ⚠️ We do not have the specific candidate-facing Box/Vimeo URL captured here. **Do not paste a specific recording URL** — offer to resend the follow-up email that contains it, or say you'll get them the link.

## Certificate eligibility — the key rule

- **Webinar certificates are issued only to people Zoom's attendance report shows attended LIVE.** This is **automatic**, generated from Zoom attendance records — it is not a personal judgment and not something we can override on request.
- Verbatim grounding from prior replies: *"According to the zoom reports, you registered but did not attend live so we can't send the certificate. This is not a decision I'm making, this is automatic based on zoom attendance records."*
- ⚠️ We don't know the exact minimum minutes Zoom requires to count as "attended." Don't quote a specific threshold. If someone insists they attended but isn't in the report, say you'll double-check the attendance record and follow up — don't promise either way.
- **Distinguish two different certificates:** (1) the **free webinar attendance certificate** (this file), vs (2) the **Practical Certificate in IECMH Consultation** earned by completing the paid program (enrollment-and-pricing.md). Don't conflate them.

## Worked examples (question → model reply)

**Q1 — "I don't see a link for the certificate. Can you help?"**
> Hi [Name] — happy to check. Webinar certificates are generated automatically from Zoom's live-attendance report and go out with the "certificate, recording and resources" follow-up. Let me confirm you're on the attendance list for the May 27 session and resend your certificate if so. I'll follow up shortly. *(Do NOT attach/promise a certificate before confirming attendance.)*

**Q2 — "Can I get a copy of the webinar recording / training?"**
> Hi [Name] — absolutely. The recording and handouts went out in the post-webinar follow-up email. I'll resend that to you so you have the link and resources in one place. Anything specific you're looking for from the session?

**Q3 — Attendee says they attended but isn't getting the certificate.**
> Hi [Name] — thanks for flagging that. Certificates are pulled automatically from Zoom's live-attendance report, so let me double-check the record for the May 27 session against your registration. If you're on it, I'll get your certificate right out; if there's a gap, I'll explain what I'm seeing. I'll follow up soon. *(Never assert they did or didn't attend until the record is checked.)*

**Q4 — "I registered but couldn't make it live — can I still get the certificate?"**
> Hi [Name] — I appreciate you asking. Because the certificate is generated automatically from Zoom's live-attendance report, I'm not able to issue one for a session you couldn't join live — it's a system rule rather than a personal call. The good news: the series is monthly, so I'd love to have you at the next one so you can earn it. Want me to send you the registration link? *(This is also a soft handoff toward the program — but if they ask to ENROLL, that's the enrollee rule.)*

**Q5 — "Can I get the slides/handouts?"**
> Hi [Name] — of course. The handouts went out with the follow-up email; I'll resend them so you have everything from the session. Let me know if anything's missing.

**Q6 — Attendee asks BOTH for the recording AND whether they can enroll in the certificate program.**
> *(Per rule priority, the enrollee rule wins — but a good combined draft:)* Hi [Name] — glad the session landed for you. I'll resend the recording and resources so you have them. And yes — it's not too late to join the Practical Certificate program; here's the short version of cost and next steps... *(continue per enrollment-and-pricing.md)*.

## Edge cases

- **Wrong session / older webinar:** if they reference a session that isn't the most recent, ask which date so we resend the correct recording/certificate.
- **Bulk request from one org** ("can you send certificates to our whole team"): acknowledge and say you'll pull the attendance report and send certificates to everyone the report shows attended live; ⚠️ may need a human to batch this.
- **Continuing-education / CEU credit question:** ⚠️ we have no documented CEU/CE-credit info. Do NOT claim the webinar offers CEUs. Say you'll check and follow up.
- **Someone asks if the webinar itself counts toward the paid certificate:** ⚠️ not documented — don't assert it does. Offer to connect them with details.

## Newly found patterns (v2 — from full Nov 2025–May 2026 scan)

**The follow-up has a richer standard bundle than v1 captured.** Real subject lines seen each month: "Your [date] certificate, recording, and tools", "Missed [date]? Here's the recording and tools", "Your Toolkit for Challenging Behaviors" / "Your Challenging Behaviors Toolkit", "Tools for your classroom", "Cloud Recording … is now available". So the post-webinar email reliably includes a **certificate (if eligible)**, the **recording**, and a **toolkit / classroom tools**. When someone asks for "materials", "handouts", "the toolkit", or "tools", treat it the same as a recording/resources request and offer to resend the follow-up.

**"Missed it live" is a frequent framing.** Many people write after missing the live session asking for the recording — note the certificate-eligibility rule still applies (recording yes, certificate only if they attended live). The "Missed [date]?" email exists specifically for them, so it's easy and on-brand to send the recording while gently explaining the certificate rule.

**Materials/printing requests from the team side** (e.g. "Today's Handouts", "Please send materials", "webinar numbers and printouts", "When you provide posters could you not have a black background?") usually come from internal/instructor folks — those should be caught by the Internal rule (priority 3), not auto-answered here. Only treat as a webinar-attendee request when it's clearly an external attendee asking for their copy.

## Hard "do nots"

- ❌ Never promise/attach a certificate before confirming live attendance in the Zoom report.
- ❌ Never paste a specific Box/Vimeo recording URL we can't verify — offer to resend the follow-up.
- ❌ Never quote a Zoom attendance-minute threshold we don't actually know.
- ❌ Never claim CEU/CE credit.
- ❌ Don't conflate the free webinar certificate with the paid program certificate.
